Writing - sentence types

  1. simple : 1 indepent clause
    • Layla studied biology. 或 => My friend Layla studied biology in university.
    • Even with the weather being that nasty, the couple and their families decided to go ahead with the wedding as planned.
      • Phrase : Even with the weather being that nasty
      • S: the couple and their families
      • V: decided
      • O: to go ahead with the wedding as planned
  2. compound : 2 indepent clauses join by conjuction
    • I arrived at the office at 9, and my assistant came 10 minutes later.
    • She was sick so I told her to go home.
  3. complex : 1 ind + 1 (more) dependent + noun/adj/adv clauses
    • John retired when he turned 65.
      • adv clause : when he turned 65.
    • Whether you agree with me or not makes little difference to our investors, who, by the way, are the ones most affected by whatever mistakes we make.
      • S : whether you agree with me or not
      • V : makes
      • adj clause (describing investors) S : who; V: are
      • noun clause : whether mistake we make
  4. compound-complex :
    • Bill voted against the measure because he felt that it wasn’t strong enough, but he also offered to continue discussions, which we will do next week.
      • S + V : Bill … the measure
      • adv clause : because he felt
      • noun clause : that it wasn’t strong enough
      • adj clause : which we will do next week

Other

  • What Shron forgot to mention was that her husband was the CEO of Microsoft and makes a lot of money, which is why she can afford all of her holidays.
    • S (noun clause, dependent clause) : What Sharon forgot to mention
    • V : was
    • SC : that her husband was the CEO of Microsoft and makes a lot of money,
    • C : which is why she can afford all of her holidays.

Inversion (v before s)

除了問句 e.g. Are you sure? 倒裝句的使用時機 - 特定句子才能使用

Negative :

  • Not only did he win, but he also broke the record.
  • Under no circumstances should you call her.
  • … nor do we expect him to

Postive :

  • Should (If) you need any help, … (should 就像是正式點的 if)
  • Had (If) I known you were coming, … (if I had known you were coming…) (縮短及更正式)
  • John speaks Chinese, as does Lucy. (so does Lucy = Lucy does as well) (縮短)
  • More important than love is money. (money is more important than love).
  • Here comes Jane. (Jane come here) (更 natural)
  • Man, is it cold out! (強調你的感覺)

Expressions with way

  • a two-way street
  • get out of (someone’s) way
  • by the way,
  • go all the way : have sex (slang in movie or life) 或想要完成某事
  • to be in a bad way : feel bad
  • to have a way with sth/sb : very good at sth/sb
  • way off (the mark) : 大大地誤解某個事實 e.g. You are way off.
  • make way : 借過; moving forward
  • way out there : crazy idea; 做白日夢
  • be on your way : (正式) leave e.g. Excuse me, can you please be on your way?
  • be on my way : (正式) I’m coming over.
