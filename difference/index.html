January 08, 2017

by / with is used to answer “how”

  • by + Ving
    • I learn English by watching YouTube.
  • by + N (transportation and communication) without any article (a, the):
    • I’ll go by bus.
    • I’ll contact you by email.
  • with + N(body parts, tools, instrument)
    • I think with my brain.
    • I cleaned the floor with a mop.
    • I ate dinner with a fork.

raise vs rise vs arise

  • raise, raised, raised (Object required) : They raised the price
  • rise, rose, risen (No Object): Please rise for the national anthem.
  • arise, arose, arisen (No Object): A few problems have arisen since I started the project

Wish vs Hope

  • hope 希望
    • I hope (that) I’ll pass the test.
    • Mary hoped (that) she wouldn’t be late for school.
  • wish 祝.. 這種情況不接 that 子句
    • I wish you good luck
    • They wished me a pleasant journey.
  • wish 希望(事與願違)
    • I wish I had passed the test.
    • I wish (that) I were you.
    • I wish it were finished.
    • I wished I hadn’t been late.
    • Mary wished she had have a baby.k
  • wish = if only (e.g.) If only I were you
  • wish 有時候也可以當作想要 = want (e.g.) Study harder if you wish to get better grades.

to vs for

  • to 幾乎前面會有一個動作的動詞，並且形容一個特定的東西 invite her to the party

  • for 算是一種結果, 目的，中間過程不管 invite her for the party

  • to: transfer / exchange (e.g.) I gave the book to my sister.

  • for: benefit (e.g.) Yogurt is good for you diestion.

  • for: help (e.g.) Could you carry these books for me?

  • for: period (e.g.) for two years

  • for: schedule / certain day or time (e.g.) I made an appointent for May 3.

  • for: agree with (e.g.) Are you for or against the development of nuclear weapons?

  • for: motive/why/a reason to do sth (e.g.) Let’s go out for a drink.

example :

  • I made this lesson for you.
  • I gave the beer to you.
  • I’m going to talk to you.
  • I will do that for you.
  • I sing for you.
  • I go to school.

s or not s

  • He stole millions of dollars.
  • I have 25 million dollars.

“go home” or “go to home”

不加 to : abroad, underground, uptown, downtown, there, here, anywhere, nowhere, somewhere, in, inside, out, outside, upstairs, downstairs

by vs until ( + time)

  • by + time 是在這時間內之前都可以 (e.g.) by 7 = no later than 7
  • until + time 是只能在最後這個時間 (e.g.) until 7 = 7 at the latest

can vs be able to

大部份情況可以通用，但不同的是用 be able to 來表達很難完成的事情

e.g.

I was able to complete the impossible mission.
Are you able to play Beethoven？

do you know what time is it?
do you know what time it is?    grammatically correct

Adj-ed vs Adj-ful

  • “Delighted” is the way you feel - very happy.
  • “Delightful” is the quality of something that makes people happy.
