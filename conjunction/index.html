Conjunction

  • With … = because of … (e.g.) With exams approaching, it’s a good idea to review your class notes. 隨著考試臨近，復習一下你的課堂筆記是個不錯的主意。
  • Once … 一旦
  • For …

though / although / even though

  • adverb clause (S+V)
  • show contrast
  • though = although
  • though is more informal
  • end this sentence with though (not although)
  • event though : emphasis / expectation

example :

  • Although the exam was difficult, I think I did well.
  • = (no comma) I think I did well although the exam was difficult.
  • = (no comma, informal) I think I did well though the exam was difficult.
  • (informal, speaking) The exam was difficult. I think I did well, though.
  • Even though the exam was easy, I failed.

despite / in spite of

  • + phrase (no verb / ving)
  • preposition + noun
  • show contrast
  • despite = in spite of

example :

  • Despite his best efforts, the man just couldn’t succeed.
  • In spite of his best efforts, the man just couldn’t succeed.
  • Despite being on time to the lecture, we were not admmitted.

therefore, thus, consequently, so, then, hence, as a result

  • therefore : premise (前提), conclusion
  • thus : result
  • consequently : consequence, result
  • so : consequence
  • then : consequence
  • hence : conclusion
  • as a result : (有點正式)

example

  1. I’m cold, so I’ll put on a coat.
  2. Sales shrank and the competition was increasing its market share.
