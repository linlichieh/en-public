Causative verbs : make, have, let, get, help

Active : S + CV + Agent + Verb + Object

  • have / make / let + V
  • get + to V

examples

  • She made me (wash) the dishes. (forced)
  • My dad let me (go) by myself. (gave permission)
  • He had the students (do) the assignment. (requested / asked / paying sb to do sth)
  • She got me (to mow) the lawn. (convinced / persuaded)
  • Engvid has helped me (可省略to) improve my English. (aided)
  • Same made her boyfriend cut his hair.
  • Cal let his friend borrow his car.
  • Jane got her sister to sew her a dress.
  • You should have the school call the boy’s parents.
  • What a cute puppy! Can you get it to do any tricks yet?

Passive : S + CV + Object + V(p.p.) + agent(optional)

  • have / get

examples

  • I had my hair cut. (cut is p.p.)
  • I had my homework done for me.
  • I got my car fixed.
  • Bill had his house painted.
  • I get my groceries delivered.
  • Your car is a mess. If you want, I can recommend a great place where you can have it cleaned inside and out, and it’s not too expensive. I bought a gorgeous black dress and new shoes, and I’m getting my hair done at Silvio’s place.
